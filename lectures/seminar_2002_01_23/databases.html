Databases in Biology


A database requires a formal data model

  • Structures (in a wide sense): comparatively easy to formalize
  • Sequences
  • Protein 3D structures
  • Taxonomic relationships
  • But: physiology, cell biology?

Biological processes are more difficult to formalize

  • Temporal phenomena are problematic in general
  • Very few examples of temporally defined databases in biology
  • Development of C. elegans, mouse: from embryo to adult
  • Pathway DB's: chemical reactions (temporal?)

Gene Ontology is a step in this direction...
